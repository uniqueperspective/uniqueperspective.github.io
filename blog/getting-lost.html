Why Getting Lost Might Be More Valuable Than Efficient Navigation

December 30, 2025

Start with a personal anecdote of a memorable time you got lost and what you discovered—a hidden café, an unexpected conversation, a neighborhood you’d never have found otherwise.

The Efficiency Trap

Explore how GPS and optimization have made us more efficient but less observant. We arrive at destinations but miss the journey. Discuss the concept of “functional fixedness” in navigation—we only see the route, not the surroundings

What Getting Lost Teaches Us
∙ Resilience and problem-solving: Real-time adaptation without the safety net
∙ Presence: Forced attention to your environment
∙ Serendipity: The unplanned discoveries that become the best memories
∙ Spatial intelligence: Actually learning geography rather than following blue lines
The Deeper Metaphor
Connect physical navigation to life navigation. Our obsession with having everything mapped out, optimized, and efficient leaves no room for the detours that define us. Some of the best things in life come from wrong turns.
Practical Application
How to intentionally get lost: monthly “wandering walks,” turning off GPS occasionally, saying yes to unexpected invitations, traveling without rigid itineraries.
Closing Thought
Efficiency gets you where you’re going. Getting lost helps you discover where you actually want to be.
